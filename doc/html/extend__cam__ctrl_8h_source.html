6  This is the sample code for Leopard USB3.0 camera use v4l2 and opencv for
7  camera streaming and display.
8 
9  Common implementation of a v4l2 application
10  1. Open a descriptor to the device.
11  2. Retrieve and analyse the device's capabilities.
12  3. Set the capture format(YUYV etc).
13  4. Prepare the device for buffering handling.
14  when capturing a frame, you have to submit a buffer to the device(queue)
15  and retrieve it once it's been filled with data(dequeue)
16  Before you can do this, you must inform the cdevice about
17  your buffer(buffer request)
18  5. For each buffer you wish to use, you must negotiate characteristics with
19  the device(buffer size, frame start offset in memory), and create a new
20  memory mapping for it
21  6. Put the device into streaming mode
22  7. Once your buffers are ready, all you have to do is keep queueing and
23  dequeueing your buffer repeatedly, and every call will bring you a new
24  frame. The delay you set between each frames by putting your program to
25  sleep is what determines your fps
26  8. Turn off streaming mode
27  9. Unmap the buffer
28  10. Close your descriptor to the device
29

31  Last edit: 2019/04
